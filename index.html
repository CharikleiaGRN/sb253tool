    Key definitions

    1) Categorical exemptions
    • Non-profit or charitable organizations that are tax-exempt under the Internal Revenue Code
    • A business entity that is subject to regulation by the Department of Insurance in this state, or that is in the business of insurance in any other state
    • A business entity whose only business in California is employee compensation or payroll expenses, including teleworking employees
    • A business entity whose only activity within California consists of wholesale electricity transactions
    • Federal, state, local government entity or majority-owned (>50.00%) by a government entity
    2) U.S.-based entity

    Entity formed under the laws of this state, the laws of any other state of the United States or the District of Columbia, or under an act of the Congress of the United States.

    3) “Doing business in California”

    Doing business and meeting either of the criteria set forth in subsections 23101(b)(1) or 23101(b)(2) of the California Revenue and Taxation Code.

    Doing business in California, as defined in § 96072, means the entity meets any of the following:

    • Actively engage in transactions for financial gain within California;

    OR

    • Are organized or commercially domiciled in California;

    OR

    • Have California sales, property or payroll exceed the following amounts under California Revenue and Taxation Code § 23101(b)

    California sales exceeding $757,070 or 25% of total sales; or

    California property exceeding $75,707 or 25% of total property; or

    California payroll/compensation exceeding $75,707 or 25% of total compensation.

    4) Revenue threshold

    Revenue is described using a gross-receipts concept and applicability is determined based on the lesser of the entity’s two previous complete fiscal years of revenue.

    For § 38532, the threshold is more than $1 billion in total annual revenue.

    5) Parent-subsidiary / consolidated reporting

    Parent companies may submit consolidated reports for in-scope subsidiaries.

    A foreign parent may also report on behalf of in-scope U.S.-based subsidiaries.

    This option is available regardless of whether the parent is itself subject to HSC § 38532.

    Special case for 2026

    Some reporting entities may not have collected Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions data for the applicable preceding fiscal year as of December 5, 2024. For these entities, CARB will accept a Statement of Non-Reporting in 2026, consistent with the December 5, 2024, Enforcement Notice.
